Orthopedic Surgeon

The prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of problems with the bones, joints, ligaments, tendons,
and muscles are the focus of orthopaedic
surgeons. Some orthopaedists are generalists, while others specialize in specific body parts,
such as the hip and knee, the foot and ankle, the shoulder and elbow, the hand,
and the spine.

Cardiologists

A Cardiologist is a medical professional who treats heart disorders, high blood pressure, heart failure,
abnormalities with your heart valves and blood arteries, as well as other cardiac and vascular conditions.
To determine what's wrong, they may ask for tests including electrocardiograms, echocardiograms, and CT scans.
Cardiologists conduct physical examinations and speak with their patients about their symptoms,
medical background, and family history.
If there are other heart disease sufferers in family,
it's crucial to let the cardiologist know as this may raise the likelihood of developing a heart condition.

Dentist

Dentistry is a term that refers to dental procedures that are performed to avoid oral diseases. After pursuing a dental course,
candidates can pursue a career in research or work for the Indian Army, Navy, or Air force.
MDS graduates have the option of starting their own speciality

Gynecologist

Treatment for infertility, gynaecological diseases, vaginal infections
(vaginitis), Dysmenorrhoea, hysterectomy, pregnancy-related issues, cervix
and uterus infections (including fungal, bacterial, viral, and protozoal), Amenorrhoea (absent menstrual periods),
and urinary incontinence are all part of a gynaecologist.
Gynaecology is one of the highest-paying medical specialities in India.
